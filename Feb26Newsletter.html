Chapter Blind Auction

The winners of the five auction prizes were announced at the January chapter meeting:

  • Dick Barthel — Ride in a WW-II era radial engine airplane
  • Joe Troglio — Plasma cut EAA wall hanging
  • Greg LePine — Pair of airplane bookends
  • Tom O'Toole — Radial engine wall clock
  • Karl Kleimenhagen — Jar of honey and jar of apple butter

The auction added roughly $400 to the chapter account, largely due to Ric Woldow's generous donation of a ride in one of his radial engine airplanes. Dick was asked which airplane he's chosen:

"I'm not sure. Mary is thinking about whether she would like a ride in the Stearman. I would love for Mary to have a ride in the Stearman, but if she declines, I would like to ride in the T-6. In no way should my choices be seen as trying to downplay the tremendous accomplishment of restoration of the Broussard... I'm thrilled to have this opportunity. Are parachutes included?"

From Headquarters

In EAA HQ's Chapter Video for January, they noted that with MOSAIC now in place, their two biggest regulatory priorities are integrating drones into the airspace and transitioning to unleaded aviation fuel. They are also addressing the growing number of electric scooters and similar vehicles at OSH 2026.

Youth Programs

Scholarships

The chapter's application for a 2026 scholarship has been accepted. We expect to know by the end of February whether we will receive one.

At the January board meeting, Rob Meyer reported that Lincoln Land has been holding $2,500 from the chapter awaiting a candidate from the Peoria area. The board agreed to release $1,500 to any qualified candidate this year.

The Lewis University scholarship was also discussed. The board feels it is less effective than Lincoln Land's, since Lewis graduates tend to remain in the Chicago area. The board is leaning toward redirecting funds to Lincoln Land or flight training — discussions continue.

Air Academy

Congratulations to Samuel Spragg! One of the teens on the Cruzer build, Samuel has been accepted to the Air Academy at this summer's OSH fly-in. The chapter will cover half the cost, with a "campership" and Young Eagles credits from 2025 helping offset the rest.

Airplane Building

Cruzer Build — Nearly Complete

The avionics have been powered up and are being configured and tested. The seats are in, and the aft cabin side glazing is being installed. Work has begun on the windscreen and cabin top glazing.

The project has not yet found a buyer. Pricing has been challenging — record keeping across three treasurers over four years has been difficult to reconcile. Treasurer Mike Simmons and Karl Kleimenhagen are close to sorting it out. The board agrees the sale will come in below total expenditures, but that the intangible benefits — youth engagement and adult involvement in building — have been well worth it.

Future builds will be limited to the airframe, with engine and avionics installed only if supplied by the buyer.

New RV-10 Project

Sudheer Sajja and Sai Suchith Pesala from Pringle Robotics have purchased an RV-10 kit with about ten kids interested in building it. They will invite teens from the current Cruzer project to join, plan to become chapter members, and will use hangar space for the work. Cruzer mentors will be available to assist.

On the Web

MGL Avionics has a new U.S. distributor: Legit Avionics in Lafayette, IN.

AvBrief continues to grow. A recent feature covered Piperzilla — a modified Cherokee 180 with vortex generators and other upgrades that is outperforming some Super Cubs in STOL competitions. Your spam can might have more potential than you think.

AvBrief also reviewed electronic ignition systems. Of particular interest is an upcoming magneto from Champion — a fully self-contained electronic ignition system, including its own internal power generator, packaged to fit a conventional magneto pad. It should generate a spark down to 100 RPM.
